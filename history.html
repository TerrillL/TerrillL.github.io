Silicon & Synapse (1991-1993)

  • Founded in 1991 by Michael Morhaime, Frank Pierce, and Allen Adham.
  • Originally Concentrated on the creation of game ports for other studios.
  • In 1993 they began developing their own software

Chaos Studios, Inc (1993-1994)

  • In December of 1993, Razer & Synapse had changed their name to Chaos Studios.

Blizzard Entertainment (1994-        )

  • In May 1994, Chaos Studios, Inc had changed their name to Blizzard Entertainment shortly after being acquired by Davidson and Associates.
  • In November 1994, Warcrafts: Orcs & Humans is released, the first game under the Blizzard Entertainment label and the first game to their Warcraft Universe
  • In October 1995, Blizzard Entertainment launches Blizzard.com, their official website.
  • In December 1996, Diablo is released and debuts as the #1 selling PC game.
  • In 1997, they launched their online gaming community Battle.net, which has 1.5 million users by the end of the year
  • In March 1998, StarCraft, the first game in the StarCraft universe, is released.
  • In December 2000, Vivendi acquires Blizzard Entertainment and Universal Studios and becomes part of Vivendi Universal.
  • In November 2004, World of Warcraft is released and breaks all previous sales records on it's first day of release, becoming the company's ninth #1 selling game.
  • In July 2008, Activision and Vivendi Games merge under the name Activision Blizzard.
