Overwatch

Overwwatch is a team based multiplayer online first-person shooter game that was released in May 2016 and has won over 100 "Game of the Year" awards across critics and reader polls. With Blizzard's focus on gameplay first, they have been telling the story of Overwatch through comics and animated shorts.

Overwatch had a numerous periods of open beta while it was still in the workds. These periods of open beta were heavily covered by the media and attracted 9.7 million players. The week prior to the game launching, they had 7 million players and by mide June following it's release, had a player base of 10 million. As of October 2017, Overwatch has a player base of 35 million players.

Overwatch League

The Overwatch League was announced during Blizzcon 2016 with the intention to spark more interest into Esports from spectators and potential sponsorships through new activites around supporting a local team. The League itself is using an organization of permanent teams similar to how athletic sport leagues rather than the use of regulation and promotions similar to League of Legion Championship Series.

The first season kicks off in January 2018 with the preseason starting in December 2017 and the Championship Tournament in July 2018. Currently there are 12 teams in the League and the breakup is as followed: 9 United States, 1 United Kingdom, 1 China, and 1 South Korea. A few United States teams are the "Boston Upgrising", which is owned by Robert Kraft, the owner of the "New England Patriots", and the "Los Angelese Gladiators", which is owned by Stan Korenke, the owner of the "Los Angeles Rams" and "Denver Nuggets".
